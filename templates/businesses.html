Advice for In-person Businesses and Organizations


Purpose

To enable enable businesses and organizations to simultaneously achieve the following objectives:

  1. Minimize cases of severe illness due to COVID-19.
  2. Enable efficient use of protective equipment.
  3. Resume in-person activities.


Motivation

SARS-CoV-2 severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2
, the virus that causes
COVID-19 coronavirus disease 2019
, can be spread by infected individuals
before they have symptoms presymptomatic
or
without any symptoms asymptomatic
[CDC]. There is no cure for COVID-19. The best defense is to avoid becoming infected, particularly for those that are at higher risk for severe illness.

Currently available non-pharmaceutical COVID-19 interventions are:

  1. COVID-19 PCR Tests (Swab tests).
  2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) at the protection level of
    N95respirator masks N95 masks should be prioritized for healthcare workers in direct contact with COVID-19 patients.
    , such as
    a washable cloth mask with a reusable face shield cap/visor A cloth mask and a face shield should be used together for higher protection.
    .
  3. Recognized Social Precautions to prevent the spread of SARS-CoV-2, as follows:
    • social distancing of at least 6 feet,
    • hand-washing for at least 20 seconds with a non-antibacterial soap,
    • use of hand sanitizers with at least 60% alcohol content,
    • use of face coverings, and
    • 14-day quarantine guidance for infected individuals.
U.S. public health decision-makers are relying on three types of policies to control the spread of the virus: lockdown , contact tracing and testing, and total compliance by the U.S. public with recognized social precautions.

Lockdown can successfully minimize infections, which in turn, minimizes cases of severe illness, but does not make efficient use PPE, and does not enable in-person activities to resume.

Contact tracing and testing is insufficient in the U.S. to control the spread of virus and does not make use of PPE, but under the paradigm of contact tracing, in-person activities have been permitted to resume.

Total compliance with precautions has been unsuccessful in the U.S., with many individuals questioning why they should comply. Further, CDC guidance creates confusion by implying that social distancing is not needed when wearing face coverings, but distancing is still important, and precautions should be used together. As a consequence, this policy has not controlled the spread of virus and does not make efficient use of PPE, but has partly permitted in-person activities to resume.

Recommended Approach

Utilize the questionnaire below to identify staff and patrons that are COVID-19 key contact individuals. This questionnaire is most appropriately completed together by a household in order to account for family interactions. No data are collected by this online questionnaire.

Individuals should follow the guidance provided by the questionnaire:

  • COVID-19 key contact individuals should comply with
    social distancing 6 feet
    and
    personal hygiene Handwashing and use of hand sanitizer
    and should be provided higher level of protection in the event of
    close contactsWithin 6 feet
    , such as installation of Plexiglas barriers and the personal use of a washable cloth mask with a reusable face shield cap/visor.
  • Baseline low-risk individuals should seek to comply with social precautions and facilitate the higher protection of key contact individuals, but do not themselves require a higher level of protection.
  • Sheltered high-risk individuals should not be present at any in-person activities.

Planning to Accommodate COVID-19 Key Contact Individuals


Small businesses or nonprofit organizations can seek free analytics assistance on planning from INFORMS Pro Bono Analytics.


Cleaning and Personal Hygiene

  1. Ensure that building ventilations systems are working properly.
  2. Conduct cleanings per CDC guidelines, particularly in areas occupied by COVID-19 key contact individuals.
  3. Conduct frequent cleaning of bathroom stalls and sinks.
  4. Ensure sufficient supply of hand soap and paper towels for handwashing.
  5. Provide availability of hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.
  6. Additional precautions can be considered, such as temperature checks and training students to cough/sneeze into their elbow.

Higher Protection for Staff

  1. Install Plexiglas barriers around the desks of COVID-19 key contact individuals. A COVID-19 key contact individual should be alone in the protected area.
  2. Provide a reusable face shield cap/visor to be worn with a cloth mask by COVID-19 key contact individuals when closely interacting with others. Affordable do-it-yourself instructions for making a face shield can be readily found online.
  3. Otherwise, maintain social distancing of 6 feet around COVID-19 key contact individuals.

Higher Protection for Patrons

The following are different options, for which only one need be implemented, but combinations could be considered, depending on what is feasible.

Option 1: Designate “key contact” sections of a facility that are zoned to enforce compliance with social distancing of 6 feet for groups that include COVID-19 key contact individuals. Other sections in which compliance with
social precautionsSocial distancing of at least 6 feet, hand-washing or hand sanitizer, face coverings
is not strictly enforced should only be occupied by baseline low-risk individuals.

Option 2: Designate “key contact” time periods or days of the week that are reserved for patrons that are COVID-19 key contact individuals. During key contact time periods, hand sanitizer should be readily available, cleaning frequency should be increased, and compliance with
social precautionsSocial distancing of at least 6 feet, hand-washing or hand sanitizer, face coverings
should be strictly enforced.

Option 3: Provide a face shield cap/visor to be worn with a cloth mask by COVID-19 key contact individuals if social distancing of 6 feet cannot be enforced. This option is only appropriate if it is feasible for the patron to wear the face shield and mask for the entire time that social distancing is not maintained. For example, this may be possible in theaters and museums, but would not be possible in restaurants.

Option 4: Install Plexiglas barriers, strategically located to protect patrons that are COVID-19 key contact individuals, where social distancing of 6-feet cannot be enforced.
