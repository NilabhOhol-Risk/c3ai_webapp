COSMOS COVID-19 Linear Programming (CC19LP)


Intended Users

County-level public health officials or decision-makers.


Purpose

To optimize planning of COVID-19 interventions:

  • Recognized Social Precautions to prevent the spread of SARS-CoV-2, as follows:
    • social distancing of at least 6 feet,
    • hand-washing for at least 20 seconds with a non-antibacterial soap,
    • use of hand sanitizers with at least 60% alcohol content,
    • use of face coverings, and
    • 14-day quarantine guidance for infected individuals.
  • COVID-19 PCR Tests (Swab tests).
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) at the protection level of
    N95 respirator masks N95 masks should be prioritized for healthcare workers in direct contact with COVID-19 patients
    such as a
    multi-layer washable cloth mask with a reusable face shield cap/visor. A multi-layer cloth mask and a face shield should be used together for higher protection.
  • COVID-19 Vaccination.
Note: The use of Plexiglas can be alternate protection option.

Goal

To enable a computationally-efficient study of the trade-off between two conflicting objectives:

  • Minimize expected daily fatality rate Estimate of the average number of fatalities per day. due to COVID-19. Note that this is highly correlated with cases of severe illness and hospitalizations.
  • Maintain normalcy of activities on a 0-10 scale Normalcy of 10 represents a return to pre-pandemic normal without social distancing, etc., and 0 represents total compliance with social precautions. due to reopening communities. Note that this is a combination of the reopening level of communities and public compliance with CDC precautions.

Approach

For a given population, identify three non-overlapping groups of individuals:

  • Group 1: Sheltered high-risk individuals that can
    isolate Shelter at home as much as possible
    themselves with the assistance of key contact individuals in Group 2.
  • Group 2: COVID-19 key contact individuals Recommended to wear personal protective equipment (PPE) when interacting with others that cannot avoid interaction with the rest of the population in Group 3 and are high-risk themselves or have a role in protecting high-risk individuals in Group 1.
  • Group 3: Baseline low-risk individualsCompliance with social precautions is at the individual’s discretion. that do not belong to Group 2.

The COVID-19 key contact group will include those that

  • work in healthcare, public safety, and other occupations that involve close interaction with both sheltered high-risk individuals and baseline low-risk individuals, and/or
  • care for sheltered high-risk individuals in their household.

All other high-risk individuals are assumed to be within the sheltered high-risk group and should shelter-in place, shielded by the COVID-19 key contact group.

All other low-risk individuals, including most children and young adults, are assumed to be within the baseline low-risk group.

In the figure, dotted lines denote protected contacts via shelter-in-place; dashed lines denote contacts requiring usage of social precautions Social distancing of at least 6 feet, hand-washing or hand sanitizer, face coverings , PPE Personal protective equipment, such as a multi-layer cloth mask with a face shield. , or testing COVID-19 PCR testing ; and solid lines denote baseline low-risk contacts. CC19LP COSMOS COVID-19 Linear Programming uses 2018 Census data to estimate the COVID-19 key contact group (Group 2) and then optimizes interventions for the key contact individuals..

User Input

State/County, COVID-19 data, Availability of COVID-19 tests and PPE/N95 masks.


CC19LP COSMOS COVID-19 Linear Programming Output

  • Estimated COVID-19 key contact group.Group 2 in the figure is further partitioned into subgroups based on risk level
  • Expected daily fatality Estimate of the average number of fatalities per day rates at various Normalcy levels Normalcy of 10 represents a return to pre-pandemic normal without social distancing, etc., and 0 represents total compliance with social precautions. for key contact individuals.
  • Optimized usage of COVID-19 interventions
    • Recognized Social Precautions
    • COVID-19 PCR Tests (Swab tests)
    • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
    • COVID-19 Vaccines
     for key contact individuals.

Steps to Follow

  • Step 1: Select State and County to study key contact group. Run Census data pull.
  • Step 2: Review estimated COVID-19 key contact group based on Census data.
  • Step 3: Enter requested COVID-19 data. Run CC19LP.
  • Step 4: Review Expected daily fatality rates at various Normalcy levels. Select Normalcy level.
  • Step 5: Review CC19LP results for selected Normalcy level to see breakdown of COVID-19 interventions by key contact type.
